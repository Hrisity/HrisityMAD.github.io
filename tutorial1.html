Tutorial 1

Welcome to your first tutorial!

Now, you will learn how to use AI tools to create a website, suitable for your design portfolio, in less than 3 hours. Here’s how to do it.

Building a design portfolio can be a bit challenging sometimes. You might be just at the beginning of your career path and lack the number of projects to add to your portfolio, or you might be a designer who worked on projects that are “confidential” and you are not allowed to share. Whatever your case is, this tutorial got you covered.

The first thing you need for your website is a topic. What the website will be about - whether it is an e-commerce website, a business website, a personal website, or an event website. Now that we have many AI tools to assist us, the easiest way to gather ideas for a potential website is to ask a chatbot for assistance. In this tutorial, we are working with ChatGPT. However, you can try working with other similar assistants such as Copilot, HuggingChat, or PopAi.

Here is a prompt that you can try out. You will notice the variable in [brackets], you can modify them so you get results that better meet your needs.

Prompt: I am a [web designer] who is working on a [portfolio website] where my [best projects will be presented]. However, I need an idea for a [website design that I can work on and add to the portfolio]. Generate a list of [website ideas, as well as what pages could be added, as well as their colour palettes].

ChatGPT: Creating a compelling portfolio website can set you apart in the web design field, showcasing your skills and creativity. Here's a diverse list of website design ideas, including potential pages and color palettes for each. These ideas are designed to demonstrate a range of your abilities, from user interface design to user experience considerations.

1. Boutique Coffee Shop Website Pages: Home, About Us, Menu, Online Order, Blog, Contact Color Palette: Warm earth tones (coffee brown, cream, burnt orange) accented with deep green for a cozy, inviting atmosphere.
2. Tech Startup Website Pages: Home, About, Product/Services, Case Studies, Careers, Blog, Contact Color Palette: Modern and minimalistic, using shades of blue, grey, and white, with neon green highlights for a tech-inspired look.
3. Travel Blog Website Pages: Home, Destinations, Travel Guides, Photo Galleries, Gear Reviews, About, Contact Color Palette: Bright and vibrant colors like turquoise, sun yellow, and sky blue to evoke a sense of adventure and discovery.
4. Online Art Gallery Website Pages: Home, Artists, Exhibitions, Virtual Tour, Events, Shop, Blog, Contact Color Palette: Neutral backgrounds (whites, light greys) with splashes of bold accent colors (like red or purple) to highlight the artwork without overshadowing it.
5. Environmental Non-Profit Website Pages: Home, About Us, Our Work, How to Help, News, Events, Contact Color Palette: Earthy and natural tones (forest green, earth brown, sky blue) paired with clean whites to communicate a connection to nature and the environment.
6. Gourmet Food Delivery Service Website Pages: Home, About Us, Menu, Subscription Plans, Gift Cards, Blog, Contact Color Palette: Rich and appetizing colors like burgundy, cream, olive green, and gold to evoke luxury and quality.
7. Flower Boutique Website Pages: Home, About Us, Shop (Categorize by occasion, flower type, etc.), Special Offers, Events, Workshops, Blog, Contact Color Palette: Primarily dark green for a lush, vibrant base, complemented by floral accents like rose pink, lavender, and butter yellow. Use gold or cream for text and accents to add a touch of luxury and elegance.
Prompt 2: I will continue with the [Flower Boutique Website]. Now generate the color palette, as well as the content for the website.

ChatGPT:For your Flower Boutique Website, focusing on a lush and elegant theme with a dark green base, I'll outline a detailed color palette and suggest content for each of the proposed pages. This approach will ensure the site not only looks visually appealing but also communicates the boutique's unique brand and offerings effectively. Color Palette

Dark Green (#0B3D2E): Serves as the primary background color, symbolizing growth and the richness of nature. Rose Pink (#E8C2CA): Accents for call-to-action buttons, links, and to highlight special offers, adding a soft, welcoming contrast. Lavender (#967BB6): Used for secondary accents, such as icons or to highlight categories, to introduce creativity and luxury. Butter Yellow (#FFD670): For promotional banners and seasonal offers, injecting warmth and optimism. Gold (#B5A642): For borders, buttons, and key headings, adding an element of elegance and quality. Cream (#FFFCEB): Background for text areas to enhance readability and provide a soft, neutral contrast against the dark green.
Website Content Home Hero Section: A captivating image of your latest or most popular flower arrangements with a strong, concise welcome message. Featured Products: Highlight seasonal offers or best-sellers here. About Us Teaser: A brief introduction to your boutique with a link to the full About Us page. Testimonials: A carousel of customer reviews for social proof. About Us Our Story: Share the history and passion behind your flower boutique, including your mission and values. Meet the Team: Profiles of your florists and staff, with photos and short bios. Sustainability Practices: Information on how your boutique sources flowers ethically and supports local growers. Shop Categories: Organized by occasion (e.g., weddings, anniversaries), flower type (e.g., roses, tulips), and price range. Filter and Sort Options: To help customers find their desired arrangements easily. Product Pages: Detailed descriptions, care instructions, and multiple images for each arrangement. Special Offers Current Promotions: Detailed information on any ongoing sales or bundle offers. Loyalty Program: If applicable, explain how customers can join and benefit from your loyalty program.

Now, that we have the structure, it is time to ask ChatGPT to create the text content. Here, you will see example prompts that you can use. Try to experiment with these.

Prompt one: For the website option, I selected, generate a list of possible names for this [online flower boutique].
Prompt two: For the website option, I selected, generate example text for the “About Us” section.
Prompt three: For the website option, I selected, generate example names for the flower bouquets.

Then, it is time to generate images for your website. In this tutorial, we are using Midjourney; however, you can use alternatives like DALL-E, ChatGPT v.4, or others.
